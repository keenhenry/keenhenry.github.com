Let's Get Bohemian!

22 Aug 2012
Tweet

I should have written down what I have seen much earlier, because I can only remember a few highlights of the trip right now.

Bohemia, land of Bohemians! That’s where I visited this summer, which is also the 20th country I have every visited. And Like every tourist, we visted Prague.

Architecturely, Prague is a beautiful city; however, the people there were not so beautiful - let me elaborate a bit what I meant by that - the waiters or waitresses did not smile much (not friendly anyhow). (My Romanian colleague told me that is quite normal for eastern european countries though).

In Prague, you can find many Art Nouveau style arcitectures, which are very beautiful; and a few Cubism style buildings. I personally found Cubism buildings the most interesting, because I had never seen anything similar elsewhere. As for modern architectures, you definitely do not want to miss the Danceing House in Prague.

Music Concerts in Prague is everywhere (well, at least in the city center). We picked two to go, and enjoyed the first one the most. The first concert was held in Municipal House, which is a beautiful and big venue to enjoy a concert. The second one was in Mirror Chapel, which features cute mirrors in the ceiling of that chapel.

The rundown of the first concert:

  1. W.A. Mozart - Oboe concerto C major
  2. F.Mendelssohn-Bartholdy - Violin Concerto E minor
  3. Antonín Dvořák - Symphony No.9 E minor “From the New World”

To enjoy your nights in Prague, you must try boat-rowing. So we did! And it was on river Moldau (Vltava)!

Other highlights include Prague Castle and the Bone Church in Kutna Hora. Prague Castle is a must see although you will certainly meet lots of tourists there, especiall in the summer!

Dekui (Thanks in Czech)!
